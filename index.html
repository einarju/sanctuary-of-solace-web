Sound Healing Therapy is one of the oldest forms of healing known to mankind. Almost every culture has used sound and vibration for healing and transformation, from Ancient Egypt, Greek, Mayans, Babylonians, Romans, Native Indigenous of the Americas, China, Aboriginal, Celts and more.

Ancient Cultures and Sound Healing

  • Aboriginal Australian Culture: Around 40,000 years ago, Aboriginal Australians used the didgeridoo to heal various ailments, making it one of the earliest documented cultures to use sound healing.
  • Tibetan and Indian Traditions: Monks in Tibet and Nepal used singing bowls in spiritual and healing ceremonies. Yogic traditions incorporate mantras and bija (seed syllables) to stimulate organ systems and promote healing.
  • Greek Philosophy: The ancient Greeks, including Pythagoras, explored the therapeutic effects of music and sound, using harmonious musical intervals to promote balance and health.
  • Shaman Medicine People: Shamans use sound healing to induce trance states for healing and spiritual journeys by employing instruments like drums and rattles to create rhythmic vibrations.

What is Shamanism?

Shamanism is a way of life, Healing and Ceremony. The name Shaman comes from the Tungus region of Siberia. It is the oldest spiritual practice dating back 400,000 years to Africa. The Shaman is a keeper of the Mystery teachings and is awake to the ever-changing world, in touch with the invisible realms.

The Shaman is in the business of restoring harmony to the individual, his/her family, society or environment. Shamanism is rooted within the forces of nature and the four directions. The Shaman is a fulcrum between heaven and earth.

What is Andean Shamanism?

In Andean Shamanism, we learn about the collective belief system and the power of nature, how to communicate directly with the Kawsay Pacha (living energy). The role of the Shaman is to deepen their relationship to our Mother who feeds and clothes us and provides our homes - Pachamama, the Cosmic and Earth Mother.

The Andeans do not use the word "Shaman", instead, they use the name PAQO meaning Holy Man/Woman, an earth Priest or Medicine Healer.

The Three Distinct Levels of Andean Cosmos:

  • Hanaq Pacha – Superior/Upper world, inhabited by highly refined beings and energies, Masters and Angelic Consciousness
  • Kay Pacha – Middle World, the space we inhabit at the 3D level, including all material things, refined energy and heavy energies
  • Ukhu Pacha – Lower world, the interior underworld which resides inside the earth consisting of the collective unconscious

What is a Shaman's role?

  • To bring harmony and balance to the areas of dissolution and chaos
  • To work in service to Mother Earth and the Cosmic Mother, Pachamama

  • To heal and clear soul sickness

The Shaman's path teaches:
